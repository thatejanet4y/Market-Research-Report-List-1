<p><p>The commercial vehicle braking systems market is primarily categorized into drum brakes and disc brakes. Drum brakes consist of a cylindrical drum that houses brake shoes, providing effective stopping power and heat dissipation, making them suitable for heavy loads. In contrast, disc brakes use a flat, circular metal disc and calipers to clamp down, offering superior performance, faster cooling, and reduced maintenance. Both systems have distinct applications, influencing their adoption based on vehicle type and operational requirements.</p></p>
