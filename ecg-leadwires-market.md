Angled leadwires feature connectors that facilitate easy attachment, reducing the risk of misplacement during procedures. J-shaped leadwires are designed for enhanced flexibility, allowing for comfortable positioning of electrodes on the patient. Straight leadwires, on the other hand, provide a more traditional approach with a linear connector design, suitable for standard use in various clinical settings. These variations cater to different clinical needs and improve patient monitoring effectiveness.</p></p>
