<p><p>The bone densitometer market consists of several types used for assessing bone density and diagnosing conditions like osteoporosis. DEXA (Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry) is the gold standard, offering precise measurements through low-radiation X-rays. Ultrasound densitometry uses sound waves to evaluate bone strength, providing a portable and radiation-free option. QCT (Quantitative Computed Tomography) delivers 3D imaging for detailed analysis of bone density. Other methods may include MRI-based techniques and peripheral devices, each serving specific clinical needs based on accuracy and application.</p></p>
