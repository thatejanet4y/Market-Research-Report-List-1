<p><p>The neurological monitoring device market can be categorized into non-invasive and invasive types. Non-invasive devices, such as EEG and fMRI, measure brain activity without penetrating the skin, making them safer and more comfortable for patients. In contrast, invasive devices, like intracranial pressure monitors, require surgical procedures to access the brain, providing more precise data for critical conditions. Both types play crucial roles in diagnosing and managing neurological disorders, guiding treatment decisions, and monitoring patient outcomes effectively.</p></p>
