<p><p>Stroke insurance encompasses various market types based on the nature of strokes: Ischemic and Hemorrhagic. Ischemic strokes, caused by blood clots, dominate the market due to their prevalence, leading to higher insurance claims and demand for treatment coverage. Conversely, Hemorrhagic strokes result from bleeding in the brain and, while less common, tend to be more severe and require substantial medical intervention. Both markets reflect the need for tailored insurance policies that cater to different treatment protocols and recovery scenarios.</p></p>
